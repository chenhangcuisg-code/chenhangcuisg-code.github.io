Chenhang Cui

Building trustworthy, aligned and safe large vision-language models.

I am a first-year PhD student at the NExT++ Research Centre, School of Computing, NUS, advised by Prof. Chua Tat-Seng. Previously I received my B.Eng. (Honors) from UESTC, and worked with Prof. Huaxiu Yao (UNC) and Prof. Lifang He (Lehigh).

My research focuses on hallucination, safety, and RL-based alignment in vision-language models, with previous work on multi-view representation learning.

# Research Interests

Trustworthy LVLMs

Hallucination, jailbreak, adversarial robustness, and safety evaluation of large vision-language models.

Alignment & RL

Preference modeling, fine-grained verifiers, RLHF / DPO for multimodal alignment.

Multi-view Representation

Consistency & complementarity in multimodal representation learning for downstream tasks.
